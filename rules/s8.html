Springfield Battle League · Season 8

Rules & Resources

Everything you need to draft, battle, and compete this season.

The Springfield Battle League is a VGC Draft League (4v4 doubles) played in Pokémon Showdown and Pokémon Scarlet and Violet. Each player drafts a team from a shared pool and plays one match per week across a ten-week regular season, followed by a postseason bracket.

Format: [Gen 9] VGC 2024 Reg G - select this when building your team and challenging your opponent. Matches are reported by 11:59 pm Sunday each week.

Signups: The league begins with signups! Interested players should post in the signups channel in the Discord or DM jdawgin13 to be added to the roster.

02Seeding & Schedule▾

Once the roster is finalized, players are seeded based on performance in past SBL seasons. Where data on a player is limited, the commissioner determines seeding from the available information.

Seeding is used to build the schedule. Because of the number of entrants, not everyone plays everyone - instead, seeding gives every player a roughly equally challenging season. Each player plays one match per week over ten weeks, facing ten different opponents. Schedules can be viewed in the Schedule & Results tab.

03The Draft▾

After seeding is set, the draft order is decided randomly and announced. All players draft from the same pool, and the draft runs snake-style: picks go down the order, then reverse at the end and come back up, repeating until every player has a full team of ten Pokémon.

Draft order & swaps
  • Two players may swap positions in the draft order if both agree. Each player may swap only once.
  • If a player swaps with someone who later drops before teams are drafted, they return to their original position.
Live draft & absences
  • The commissioner will try to find a time when all players can draft live. Otherwise, drafting proceeds gradually through Discord until everyone has ten Pokémon.
  • If a player who agreed to attend is absent, they automatically draft the highest-usage Pokémon (per Pikalytics) from the highest tier they still have open.
Draft timer
  • Any player may request a turn timer at any point during a match - 2 minutes per turn by default.
  • Players may agree to a different time; if they can't agree, the 2-minute default applies.
  • The timer is kept by a third party (preferably jdawg or Shrug), displayed if possible, otherwise announced regularly.
Opening swap window: For the first 24–48 hours after the draft (the exact length is announced), players may make unlimited trades and Free Agency swaps. These are processed effective Week 1, so anything picked up in this window is available immediately.

05In-Match Rules▾
Team selection
  • 4v4 double battle format. Each player brings 6 Pokémon per match and selects 4 to use each game.
  • Once your first game of a match begins, you must use the same team of 6 for every game in that match.
  • You may pick a different set of 4 from your 6 each game, but you may not change moves, items, abilities, stats, natures, or anything else between games.
Banned moves, abilities & items
  • Moves that exclusively raise evasion or lower accuracy - Sand Attack, Double Team, Minimize, and similar - are banned (including items like Bright Powder and Mud Slap).
  • No Commander, Moody, or Booster Energy.
  • Duplicate items are not allowed.
Forfeits
  • A forfeited match results in a −2 game differential and a −4 K/D for the forfeiting player.
06Trades & Free Agency▾

Players can adjust their drafted teams through Free Agency swaps (trading for undrafted Pokémon) or trades with other players.

  • Outside the opening window, each player gets up to 10 swaps and trades total for the season.
  • No trades or swaps are allowed after Week 8.
  • All transactions are processed effective the week after they're announced - e.g. a pickup announced in Week 3 is usable in Week 4. This gives opponents time to prepare.
  • Announce all swaps and trades in the #sbl-transactions channel on Discord.

(The opening 24–48 hour window after the draft is the exception - those are unlimited and effective Week 1. See The Draft.)

08All-Star Match & Awards▾
All-Star Match

An optional, for-fun side event. Players vote on the season's true All-Star Pokémon, which are split into two groups led by chosen team captains. Players present in Discord are picked schoolyard-style onto each team, given time to build, and face off in a best-of-five set. Pokémon can be swapped each game, but their movesets, stats, and so on cannot change. It's entirely optional but very much worth joining.

09Competitive Integrity & Enforcement▾
  • Sabotage of matches - match fixing, intentionally losing, or otherwise undermining the league's competitive integrity - is not allowed.
  • Spectators are welcome but should avoid giving advice or information, intentionally or accidentally, to players in ongoing matches.
  • Helping players prepare for upcoming matches is fine - but don't build a complete Poképaste for another player. The sharing restriction applies specifically to ongoing matches.

Violations are punished based on their nature and the commissioner's judgment, ranging from a warning to removal from the league. Anything not covered here is resolved case-by-case. Reach out to the commissioner for clarification on any rule.
